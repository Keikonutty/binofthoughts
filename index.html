Step 1

Choose the wood for your board, we recommend birch it is very flexible and it's also very durable Types of wood: Maple wood is really heavy and sturdy, mostly used for downhill riding. Only downside to maple is that its really heavy and this can be a problem to some riders who carry their board everywhere. Bamboo wood is very flexible and light, it is used for cruising since it easily rides over bumps and rough ground. Bamboo boards are also very good at turning. Birch wood is very flexible like bamboo and it has sturdiness like maple wood, it is used for riding anywhere, it can handle downhill speeding and also can cruise very well

Step 2

Choose your bearings, bearings are rated by ABEC the higher the ABEC rating the more precise and accurate the bearing will be. The ABEC ratings are 1,3,5,7,9 ABEC 1 bearings are the least expensive, and the worst crude, the quality of the steel is not very high ABEC 3 bearings are generally inexpensive and do not roll smooth or quickly ABEC 5 bearings are standard bearings for most types of skateboarding, ABEC 5 bearings provide a fast speed bearing at an affordable price. ABEC 7 bearings are very fast and smooth, they cost slightly more than ABEC 5 ABEC 9 bearings are the fastest bearings of them all, these are the best for downhill skating and skaters who want to cruise very fast

Step 3

Choose your trucks, if you are wanting to speed downhill then you may want to buy Caliber trucks, they are the all around best buy Another set of really good trucks are Paris trucks, they are high quality and are easy to adjust If you are looking for the cheapest trucks you should buy Yoacher longboard trucks, these are perfect for low cost setups if you pair them with a quality deck
